### 2. Product Roadmap

**Purpose:** Track features, prioritize work, and communicate direction.

**Key sections:**
- Feature backlog with priority scoring
- Quarterly roadmap
- Sprint planning
- Stakeholder updates
- KPI tracking

**Best practices:**
- Use RICE scoring (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) for prioritization
- Share company-wide for transparency
- Update quarterly, not daily

**Pro tip:** Add an "ideas graveyard" to show what you've considered and why you said no.

---

### 3. Investor Updates

**Purpose:** Keep investors informed and build trust through regular updates.

**Structure:**
- Monthly metrics (MRR, growth, churn)
- Key accomplishments
- Challenges faced
- Requests (intros, feedback, hires)
- Financials (burn rate, runway)

**Frequency:** Monthly for angels, quarterly for VCs.

**Why it matters:** Investors who receive regular updates are 3x more likely to invest in your next round.

**Template tip:** Use a table so investors can quickly scan trends over time.

---

### 4. User Research Repository

**Purpose:** Centralize customer insights and inform product decisions.

**Sections:**
- Interview transcripts
- User personas
- Pain point database
- Feature requests tracker
- Win/loss analysis

**Setup:**
- Tag each insight by user segment
- Link insights to specific features
- Review weekly with product team

**ROI:** Teams that systematically track user insights build products 2x faster.

---

### 5. Content Calendar

**Purpose:** Plan and execute content marketing consistently.

**Components:**
- Editorial calendar (3 months out)
- Topic ideas bank
- Draft status tracking
- Publishing schedule
- Performance metrics

**Workflow:**
1. Brainstorm topics → Topic bank
2. Assign writers → Calendar
3. Draft → Review → Publish
4. Track performance → Iterate

**Pro tip:** Include content repurposing plan (1 long-form → 5 tweets → 1 newsletter).

---

### 6. OKR Tracking

**Purpose:** Align team around measurable goals and track progress.

**Framework:**
- Objective (What)
- Key Results (How measured)
- Initiatives (What we're doing)
- Progress (Current vs. target)
- Owner (Who's responsible)

**Best practices:**
- Set 3-5 OKRs per quarter
- Make them measurable (no "vanity metrics")
- Review weekly in team meetings

**Why OKRs:** Companies using OKRs are 2x more likely to achieve their goals.

---

### 7. Hiring Pipeline

**Purpose:** Streamline recruitment and make better hiring decisions.

**Stages:**
- Candidate sourcing (where they came from)
- Application screening (resume review)
- Interviews (structured feedback)
- Decision (hire/no-hire rationale)
- Offer (status and negotiation)

**Pro tips:**
- Score candidates on consistent criteria
- Track diversity metrics
- Document interview feedback immediately
- Calculate time-to-hire

**Essential for:** Any startup hiring 2+ people per month.

---

### 8. Competitive Analysis

**Purpose:** Understand your market and identify differentiation opportunities.

**Sections:**
- Competitor list (categorized by tier)
- Feature comparison matrix
- Pricing analysis
- Positioning map
- SWOT analysis

**Update frequency:** Monthly for active markets, quarterly for stable ones.

**Strategic value:** Teams that track competitors spot market shifts 3 months earlier.

---

### 9. Meeting Notes

**Purpose:** Capture decisions, action items, and context from meetings.

**Structure:**
- Attendees
- Agenda
- Discussion points
- Decisions made (with rationale)
- Action items (owner + deadline)
- Next meeting date

**Pro tip:** Create a template for each meeting type (1:1, sprint planning, all-hands).

**Time saved:** Never wonder "what did we decide?" again.

---

### 10. Personal Founder Dashboard

**Purpose:** Track your personal effectiveness and avoid burnout.

**Metrics:**
- Daily habits (sleep, exercise, deep work)
- Weekly energy levels
- Monthly reflection
- Quarterly goals
- Annual vision

**Why it matters:** Founders who track personal metrics last 2x longer before burnout.

**Template tip:** Include a "stop doing" list to protect your time.

## How to Build Your Template System

### Step 1: Start with One

Don't try to implement all 10 at once. Pick the one that solves your biggest pain point right now.

**Red flags that you need a template:**
- "Where is that document?"
- "What did we decide last week?"
- "I forgot to send that update"
- Chaos in a specific area

**Start here:**
- Raising money? → Investor Updates
- Launching soon? → Product Hunt Launch Template
- Building product? → Product Roadmap
- Hiring? → Hiring Pipeline

### Step 2: Customize for Your Startup

Templates are starting points, not rigid systems.

**Customize:**
- Add your specific metrics
- Match your team's workflow
- Include your branding
- Remove what you don't use

**Don't customize:**
- Core frameworks (OKRs, RICE scoring)
- Best practices (weekly reviews, monthly updates)
- Data structure (dates, owners, status)

### Step 3: Roll Out to Team

**Implementation playbook:**

1. **Test it yourself** — Use it for 1 week
2. **Get buy-in** — Show value, not just features
3. **Train the team** — 15-minute tutorial
4. **Set expectations** — When to use, how to use
5. **Review after 2 weeks** — What's working? What's not?

**Pro tip:** Appoint a "template owner" to maintain and improve it.

### Step 4: Iterate and Improve

Templates should evolve as your startup grows.

**Quarterly template review:**
- What sections are unused? Delete them.
- What pain points emerged? Add sections.
- What's confusing? Clarify language.
- What's missing? Add it.

**Sign it's time for an update:** You're consistently working around the template instead of with it.

## Common Template Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Over-Engineering

**The trap:** Creating 50 sections, complex databases, nested views.

**The fix:** Start simple. You can always add complexity later. 80% of value comes from 20% of features.

### Mistake 2: Not Using Templates Consistently

**The trap:** Team creates templates but doesn't use them.

**The fix:** Build template usage into workflows. Make it easier to use the template than not to.

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Template Debt

**The trap:** Templates accumulate cruft — unused sections, outdated fields.

**The fix:** Quarterly "template spring cleaning." Remove what isn't used.

### Mistake 4: One Template to Rule Them All

**The trap:** Trying to create one mega-template for everything.

**The fix:** Modular templates. One template = one job. Compose them as needed.
