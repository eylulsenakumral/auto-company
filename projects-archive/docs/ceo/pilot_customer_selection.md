# NextVision Pilot Customer Selection

**Decision Date:** 2025-06-03
**Decision Maker:** CEO (Jeff Bezos)
**Status:** Approved for outreach

## Decision Framework

### Core Question
Who will validate our business model fastest - not who is the biggest logo, but who gives us the clearest signal that EHS managers will pay $1/day for AI-powered PPE compliance?

### Customer Obsession
The customer is not the factory owner. The customer is the **EHS Manager** who:
- Gets fined when workers lack helmets
- Gets fired when auditors fail them
- Cannot watch all cameras all day
- Needs a simple solution, not a 6-month IT project

### Regret Minimization
What will we regret in 80 years?
- **NOT** "we should have waited for the enterprise"
- **YES** "we didn't talk to real customers fast enough"

## Selected Pilot Candidates (Ranked)

### Rank #1: Automotive Tier-2 Suppliers (Bursa/Odayeri)

**Segment:** Metal stamping, welding, plastic injection for automotive OEMs
**Size:** 100-300 employees
**Location:** Bursa industrial zone, 2 hours from Istanbul

**Why This Is Ideal:**
1. **Extreme regulatory pressure** - Automotive ISO certifications require perfect EHS. One failed audit = lost contracts.
2. **Existing RTSP cameras** - Tier-2 suppliers already monitor production lines for quality, we just tap into existing feeds.
3. **Decision speed** - EHS managers have autonomy under 500K budget. No CFO approval needed.
4. **High pain visibility** - PPE violations stop lines, cause rework, trigger Toyota/Fiat penalties.
5. **Geographic density** - Bursa has 200+ automotive suppliers in 20km radius. One trip = 10 prospects.

**Company Size Sweet Spot:**
- 100-300 employees = EHS manager exists but cannot watch all cameras manually
- Large enough to afford $30/month (micro-budget)
- Small enough to deploy in 1 day (no IT committee)

**Outreach Approach:**
1. **LinkedIn InMail** to "İSG Koordinatörü" (EHS Coordinator) roles in Bursa automotive
2. **Message:** "We use your existing cameras to catch missing helmets 24/7. $1 for 30 days. No new hardware."
3. **Trade show follow-up** - WIN Eurasia (June, Istanbul) - automotive manufacturing expo
4. **Referral hook** - "After 3 factories, we'll share violation benchmarks across suppliers"

**Estimated Close Time:** 5-7 days from first contact

### Rank #2: Textile Dyeing & Finishing (Bursa/Denizli)

**Segment:** Fabric dyeing, printing, finishing
**Size:** 50-150 employees
**Location:** Bursa, Denizli (textile hubs)

**Why This Works:**
1. **Chemical exposure** - Dye houses require full PPE (respirators, aprons, gloves). Violations are dangerous.
2. **Turkish textile export pressure** - EU buyers demand social compliance (RJC, OEKO-TEX). PPE violations = lost orders.
3. **Simple environment** - Dye lines have fixed stations (fewer zones to monitor).
4. **High turnover labor** - Migrant workers change frequently, training is expensive. Automation helps.

**Pain Level:** Medium-High (export pressure, not regulatory fines)

**Outreach Approach:**
1. **Email to "Teknik Müdür" (Technical Manager)** - they own safety + production
2. **Value prop:** "Stop EU buyer rejections. Auto-detect PPE gaps in dye stations."
3. **Denizli textile association** - bulk outreach to members

**Estimated Close Time:** 7-10 days

### Rank #3: Construction Materials Manufacturing (Istanbul/Kocaeli)

**Segment:** Cement, brick, insulation, gypsum
**Size:** 200-500 employees
**Location:** Kocaeli (1 hour from Istanbul), organized industrial zones

**Why This Viable:**
1. **Dangerous environments** - Heavy machinery, dust, high noise require full PPE
2. **İSG inspections are frequent** - Construction materials are high-priority for Ministry of Labor
3. **Existing safety culture** - Many already have cameras and safety protocols

**Why This Is Rank #3:**
- Longer sales cycle (200-500 employees = more hierarchy)
- Seasonal demand (construction slows in winter)
- More complex camera setups (outdoor + indoor)

**Outreach Approach:**
1. **Direct mail campaign** to "İSG Uzmanı" (Occupational Safety Specialist) roles
2. **Cold email from CEO** - "We help EHS teams scale oversight. Pilot for $1."

**Estimated Close Time:** 10-14 days

### Success Metrics

**Pilot Success = One of These:**
1. **Revenue:** At least 2/3 pilots convert to paid subscription after 30 days
2. **Retention:** 0 churn in first 30 days (they keep using it)
3. **Referral:** At least 1 pilot introduces us to another factory

**Failure Criteria (Pivot Signal):**
- 0/3 convert - but all say "we'd pay $10/month" → Pricing problem, not product
- 0/3 convert - all say "we need helmet counting, not detection" → Feature gap
- 0/3 convert - all say "we need full safety audit, not just PPE" → Positioning problem

## Non-Decisions (Rejected Segments)

### Why NOT Enterprise (>500 employees)?
- Sales cycle: 3-6 months
- IT security reviews, procurement committees
- We need learning, not prestige

### Why NOT Micro Factories (<50 employees)?
- No EHS manager (owner wears 10 hats)
- Price sensitivity (won't pay $30/month)
- PPE violations are sporadic, not systemic

### Why NOT Food & Beverage?
- HACCP priority over PPE (different compliance focus)
- Many have hygiene protocols but fewer helmet requirements
- Smaller cameras footprint

## Critical Assumptions

1. **EHS managers have $30/month budget authority** - If they don't, we need to sell to owners (longer cycle)
2. **RTSP cameras are already installed** - If <50% have cameras, we need hardware partnerships
3. **PPE violations happen daily** - If it's weekly, our value proposition weakens

### How We'll Test These
- Ask in first call: "What's your budget for safety tools?"
- Ask in discovery: "Show me your current camera setup"
- Ask in discovery: "How often do you catch missing PPE?"
